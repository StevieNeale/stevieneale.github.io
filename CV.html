Executive Bio

Stevie Neale is a cloud and IT systems engineering leader known for transforming complex enterprise environments into scalable, automated, and secure ecosystems. With experience across multi‑cloud infrastructure, identity governance, and enterprise IT operations, Stevie brings a rare combination of technical depth, cross‑functional collaboration, and people‑centered leadership.

Stevie has led high‑impact initiatives across Azure/EntraID and Okta, modernizing infrastructure, strengthening IAM controls, and automating mission‑critical workflows that support thousands of users. At Myriad Genetics, Stevie served as Scrum Master for the Identity & Access Management team, driving Agile delivery, coordinating cross‑team engineering efforts, and shaping the RBAC and access governance models used across the organization. Their work automating onboarding, offboarding, and access provisioning significantly improved operational efficiency, reduced risk, and aligned identity processes with HITRUST and compliance requirements.

Their ability to diagnose complex issues, design sustainable solutions, and communicate clearly across technical and non‑technical teams has made them a trusted partner in both engineering and security domains and led to three promotions in three years.

With a 20‑year foundation in teaching and community organizing, Stevie brings a leadership style rooted in clarity, empathy, and empowerment. They excel at guiding teams through change, translating technical complexity into shared understanding, and building systems that elevate both organizational performance and human experience. Their work consistently delivers stability, security, and strategic value in environments where reliability, integrity, and trust are paramount.

Leader and Community Organizer

Prior to Covid-19 (and parenthood), Stevie was a career artist and community leader. Graduating with a bachelors in Musical Theatre and Policial Science in 2009, it was hard to see how these dual majors would intersect. In 2013, Stevie earned their Masters in Urban Studies Community Arts and became an independent teaching artist and political activist working with several Philadelphia, New Jersey, and Delaware non-profit organizations to improve resilience and hope in vulnerable populations. They wrote grants and curriculum, fundraised, and managed teams of artists for summer and in-school programs. Some organizations Stevie worked with include: BuildaBridge International, Philadelphia Young Playwrights, Musicopia, Settlement Music School, Hopeworks, The Neighborhood Center, Urban Promise, Wolf Performing Arts Center, Zoom Dance, Yes, And…

For both career growth and financial reasons, Stevie transitioned from full-time community arts work to a career in IT and cloud systems engineering, where they could leverage their skills in leadership, problem-solving, and technology to make a broader impact. Having such a diverse background has enabled them to excel in their communication and interpersonal skills as well as provided an opportunity to be an eternal learner! Today, they continue to be an active participant in their community through volunteer work, mentorship, and advocacy for arts education. Stevie serves on the Board of BuildaBridge, a nonprofit arts organization focused on bringing trauma-informed arts education to underserved communities. They are fortunate to be the unofficial advisor for TheLaughtHer Collective, an incredible virtual comedy school for under-represented comics run by their brilliant partner.
